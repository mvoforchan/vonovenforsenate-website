Leadership When It Matters

Standing up for constitutional principles when others stayed silent

In December 2025, ICE enforcement surrounded workers on a Chanhassen rooftop in subzero temperatures. I was the only elected official to speak up. This moment crystallizes why I'm running for State Senate.

Chanhassen City Council Meeting - January 2024

Why This Moment Matters

Leadership isn't about making the loudest noise or taking the easiest path. It's about standing up for principles even when it's difficult.

When federal agents' lack of coordination with local law enforcement led to workers being detained on a rooftop in dangerous, subzero conditions, I could have stayed silent like the others. Instead, I spoke up—not because it was politically convenient, but because it was right.

What I Stood For:

  • Due process and constitutional rights for everyone
  • Proper coordination between federal and local law enforcement
  • Transparency and accountability in government operations
  • Basic human dignity and safety, regardless of citizenship status

The response from the community was overwhelmingly positive—not because everyone agreed on immigration policy, but because they recognized principled leadership when they saw it.

This is exactly the kind of leadership District 48 needs in St. Paul: someone who will stand up for constitutional principles, demand government accountability, and put people before politics.
